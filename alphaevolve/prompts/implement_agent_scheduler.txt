== DESIGN GOAL ==
Create a real scheduling algorithm, not a parameter tweak:
- combine ideas from existing XLS schedulers and literature into a hybrid heuristic
- change how nodes are prioritized / packed / spread across stages
- aim to improve generated hardware PPA by changing the schedule itself

Think in AlphaEvolve-style terms:
- produce a distinct heuristic family, not just small constant changes
- keep the algorithm deterministic and compilable
- reuse existing XLS bounds/constraint machinery rather than rebuilding all infrastructure
- prefer a coherent strategy such as pressure-aware list scheduling, mobility-aware
  placement, multi-pass refinement, or greedy schedule repair
- be innovative in the heuristic, but disciplined in implementation size and runtime
- prefer one coherent algorithmic family over a giant hybrid of many unrelated ideas
- do NOT fall back to trivial ASAP-only behavior unless that is clearly justified by
  a stronger scoring/tie-breaking scheme
- avoid bloated helper duplication, multiple abandoned scoring families, or heavy
  nested search that is likely to time out on larger designs
- bounded lookahead or one repair pass is good; unbounded multi-pass exploration is not

Use all of your relevant prior knowledge as well:
- the `knowledge/` excerpts are helpful context, not an exclusive whitelist
- you may draw on general graph algorithms, scheduling theory, compiler heuristics,
  optimization intuition, and standard hardware-scheduling ideas you already know
- if the local knowledge is incomplete, still propose a principled algorithm based
  on sound fundamentals rather than limiting yourself to only the quoted notes

Runtime expectations:
- target a practical heuristic that can run on larger graphs such as GEMM-style designs
- prefer roughly single-pass topological scheduling with bounded per-node candidate scoring
- be careful with repeated expensive recomputation after every choice
